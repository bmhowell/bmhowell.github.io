About

I am currently a PhD candidate in the Multi-physics Simulation and Optimization Laboratory at UC Berkeley where I specialize in numerical optimization for materials design. I am interested high performance computing for applied math/physics, but am generally application agnostic. More than anything, I enjoy chasing flow states powered by light-roast espresso and C++.

Previously, I worked as a staff scientist at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory where I worked on simulation, optimization, hardware and chemical formulation for 3d printing, and at Google X where I worked as an AI resident in materials optimization.

I have a variety of interests including running very long distances, foreign film, olives, olive oil, uppers (not downers), discrepancies between Talebian philosophy and Talebian tweets, Jewish atheism, 1980's Japan, 1990's New York, 2000's Silicon Valley, Indian food in England, Indian food in India, desert wealth, and sliding the word 'optimisation' into conversations.
