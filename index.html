About

I am currently a PhD candidate in the Multi-physics Simulation and Optimization Laboratory at UC Berkeley where I specialize in numerical optimization for materials design. My expertise lie in optimization, simulation, and high performance computing. More than anything, I enjoy chasing espresso-fueled flow states coding in C++. I am currently searching for a new crusade that will allow me to work at the intersection of hardware and code upon graduation in May 2024.

Previously, I was an AI Resident in materials optimization at Google X where I worked on physics-simulations, Gaussian Processes, and convex modeling/optimization. Prior to Google, I was a staff scientist at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory where I worked on simulation, optimization, hardware and chemical formulation for 3d printing. My undergraduate degree is in chemical engineering with a focus on control theory.

I have a variety of interests including running very long distances, foreign film, olives, olive oil, discrepancies between Talebian philosophy and Talebian tweets, Schumpeterian and Thielian economics, Jewish atheists, 1980's Japan, 1980's New York, 2000's Silicon Valley, Indian food in England, Indian food in India, desert wealth, and sliding the word 'optimisation' into conversations.
